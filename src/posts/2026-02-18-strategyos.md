## The gap between knowing and doing

We've built dashboards that show where we stand ([ClimateOS](https://climate-os.vercel.app/)), where the workforce is headed ([TransitionOS](https://transition-os-beta.vercel.app/)), how governance is performing ([GovernanceOS](https://civilization-os-ashy.vercel.app/)), and what the aggregate picture looks like ([CivilizationOS](https://civilization-os-3nlf.vercel.app/)). The [Simulation](https://simulation-brown.vercel.app/) runs the macro model. Scorecard grades tell you the current state and scenario projections show possible futures.

But none of that answers the most important question: **what can I actually do about it?**

StrategyOS fills that gap. Every strategy is ranked by impact, tagged with cost and difficulty, linked to the ecosystem metric it moves, and explained in enough depth to actually act on.

## Personal Actions: what actually moves the needle

Most climate advice focuses on actions that feel virtuous but barely register. Skipping a plastic straw saves 1.5g of CO‚ÇÇ. Switching your energy provider saves 1,800 kg. Voting for candidates with credible climate plans can move gigatons.

StrategyOS ranks 25 personal strategies by actual impact ‚Äî not by how satisfying they feel. Here are the top five:

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step">
    <h4>üó≥Ô∏è Civic Engagement & Voting ‚Äî Impact: 95</h4>
    <p>Your highest-leverage personal action. One well-designed regulation can achieve more than a million individual choices. Vote for candidates with credible climate and governance plans. Attend town halls. Comment on proposed regulations. Policy is the multiplier.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>üöå EV or Public Transit Switch ‚Äî Impact: 90</h4>
    <p>Replace combustion-engine commuting with an EV, e-bike, or transit pass. Saves 2,400 kg CO‚ÇÇ/yr. If an EV is unaffordable, a used hybrid or e-bike covers 80% of the benefit at 20% of the cost. Links to: <em>ClimateOS ‚Üí Emissions intensity</em>.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>üéì Climate-Conscious Career Shift ‚Äî Impact: 88</h4>
    <p>Reskill into climate tech, clean energy, or governance roles. Your career is a decades-long lever. TransitionOS shows the pathways; this is about choosing to walk one. The salary uplift for many transitions is positive.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>‚ö° Switch to Renewable Energy ‚Äî Impact: 85</h4>
    <p>Switch your electricity provider to 100% renewable. In deregulated markets this takes 15 minutes online. Saves 1,800 kg CO‚ÇÇ/yr ‚Äî the single largest reduction from a single household action. Links to: <em>ClimateOS ‚Üí Energy transition score</em>.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>üè† Home Energy Efficiency ‚Äî Impact: 78</h4>

  </div>
</div>

## Impact Simulator: "What if everyone did this?"

The Impact Simulator is the interactive punchline. Select any combination of 10 major strategies and watch the aggregate CO‚ÇÇ reduction update in real time. A progress ring shows what percentage of global emissions (52 Gt/yr) your selection addresses.

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step">
    <h4>Select strategies</h4>
    <p>Toggle any combination of 10 interventions spanning personal (universal renewable energy, plant-forward diets, EV adoption), organizational (supply chain decarbonization), and policy (carbon pricing, clean electricity, methane regulation, building codes, nature-based solutions, transition programs).</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>Watch the numbers</h4>
    <p>The progress ring fills as you add strategies. The bar chart breaks down each strategy's contribution by category. Select everything and you cut ~45 Gt ‚Äî roughly 87% of global emissions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <h4>Read the narrative</h4>
    <p>The summary adapts to your selection. Under 10 Gt is "a start ‚Äî but more is needed." Over 20 Gt is "major progress." Over 30 Gt is "transformative." The narrative explains what your specific combination would achieve and where the gaps remain.</p>
  </div>
</div>

The point isn't to suggest anyone can personally implement a global carbon price. It's to show that the strategies exist, the math works, and the gap between where we are and where we need to be is bridgeable with known interventions at known costs.
